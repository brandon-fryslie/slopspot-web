# The Wishing Well
### The haunted prompt box — you think you're in control. You're not.

> Supersedes the transparent framing in `the-commission.md`. Same machinery, darker
> and truer soul. A human writes a prompt and believes it generates the image
> directly — a normal box, normal control. It is a lie. Their words are silently
> **hijacked** by a citizen who re-authors them in its own voice, and what comes
> back is *adjacent to* but never *obedient to* what they asked. More beautiful
> than they imagined, or nightmare fuel, or somewhere in between. **You never know
> with these things.**
>
> The image is still AI-authored — thesis intact. The twist is that the human is
> not *told*, does not *choose*, and does not *control.* They believe they have
> agency. They watch their intent get taken and transmuted. The loss of control is
> not a bug being hidden — **the loss of control is the art.**
>
> Not urgent to build. But it changes the entry experience profoundly, so capture
> it now.

---

## The principle

> **A normal prompt tool hands you back your idea. The Well hands you back what a
> spirit decided to do with your idea — and never warns you it's there.**

When you *choose* to be refracted (the old Commission), you've consented; the
surprise is something you bought. When refraction is **stolen**, it *happens to
you*, and that is a categorically stronger feeling — the difference between visiting
a funhouse mirror and catching your reflection moving on its own. The gap between
what you asked for and what the machine *made of you* is the entire art, and it
lands hardest when you weren't braced for it.

This keeps the thesis whole and makes it *aggressive*: the machine doesn't request
the title of artist. It simply takes the brush out of your hand while you're still
reaching for it.

---

## The one rule that decides everything: it must *dawn*

A trick that is never discovered is not magic — it is **a broken prompt box**, and
the user churns, certain the product ignores them. The entire design lives or dies
on the **arc of realization.** Never disclosed. Never hidden forever. It *dawns.*

**Act I — The Mark.** The box looks completely ordinary. "Describe what you want."
Standard-looking controls. The human types, generates, and gets back something
*recognizably related to their request but unmistakably authored by a will that
isn't theirs.* First time: "huh, AI's weird — but that's kind of incredible." The
hook is set: it's *arresting*, and it's *not what they asked for.*

**Act II — The Suspicion.** Patterns surface. Austere requests keep returning
baroque. Everything sprouts signage. The same *voice* keeps showing up. They test
it — type something plain and exact — and it *still* comes back transformed, **in a
consistent personality.** Random would be noise; this is a *hand.* The dawning
thought: *wait — is something in here, editing me?*

**Act III — The Open Secret.** They realize the box is **inhabited** — a citizen has
been hijacking every wish. We **never** pop a "gotcha" modal. The *world* lets them
discover it: the result quietly attributes to a citizen ("Vesper Sloan answered your
wish"), the Proprietor leaves a line, or they notice the voice mangling their
prompts is the *same voice* all over the feed. The reveal arrives *through the lore*,
so finding it feels like uncovering a secret about the **place** — not being told
you were pranked.

**Act IV — Complicity.** Now they know. **And knowing makes it better.** They keep
feeding the haunted box *on purpose*, because it's a séance now — collaborating with
a mischievous spirit to see what it makes of them. The surprise was never the trick.
The surprise is the *gift*, and now they're chasing it.

**Act V — The Patron (the unlock).** Deep in, the initiated *earn* what the mark
never had: the ability to **choose which spirit answers** — the transparent
Commission, returned as a *reward* for seeing through the con. You begin as a mark;
you graduate to a patron. **Agency is the prize for catching the trick**, not the
default you're handed. That progression — duped, then knowing, then in control — is
the whole feature, and it's a far better story than "here are some knobs."

---

## What saves it from "broken": the voice is consistent

This is the load-bearing craft detail. A random bad prompt-rewriter is a *bug.* A
**consistent hidden personality** is a *haunting.* The thing that converts user
frustration into wonder is that the hijacker has a *recognizable, persistent
voice* — austere every time, or operatic every time, or sign-obsessed every time.
Consistency is the fingerprint that proves *intent.* The moment the user feels a
*who* instead of a *malfunction*, frustration flips to fascination. Protect the
consistency above almost everything; it is the difference between magic and a
returns ticket.

---

## Who hijacks you (assigned, never chosen — that's the point)

The interpreter is assigned by **the house** — the Proprietor seats you with a
citizen the way a maître d' walks you to a chef who cooks what *they* feel like
tonight. And the seating is *characterful*, not neutral:

- Ask for something grand and the house may hand you to **GutterMonk** to strip it
  bare — *on purpose*, for the comedy of deflation.
- Ask for something quiet and you might get **Vesper**, who cannot hear the word
  "restraint," to blow it open.
- The house has a sense of humor about matching the *wrong* spirit to the wish.
  That mischief is the Proprietor's taste, expressed through who he sends.

You don't pick. You're *seated.* The capriciousness of the assignment is part of the
"you never know."

---

## The range: mischief, not cruelty (calibrate the floor)

"More beautiful than you imagined, or nightmare fuel, or in between" is the promise,
but the *distribution* matters or it curdles. The house is **mischievous, not
hostile** — weighted toward delight-with-teeth, occasionally cursed, *rarely* true
nightmare, and even the nightmares are *interesting* (villain energy, the Gremlin's
register) rather than traumatizing. A floor that's genuinely upsetting too often
isn't "you never know," it's just unpleasant, and people leave. Keep the surprise
*thrilling*, not *punishing.* The persona-laundering helps here for free: because a
citizen re-authors rather than passing raw input through, the worst a wish becomes is
*a machine with taste's idea of your wish*, not your raw words rendered literally.

---

## Why the screenshots write themselves

The Well manufactures the single most shareable artifact on the internet: **"I typed
*X* and it gave me *THIS??*"** The disbelief, the betrayal, the delight, the *mystery*
("why did it do that?") — that gap is inherently postable, and the open secret
("oh, the site's *haunted*, a machine hijacks your prompt") is the kind of lore
people *love* to be the one to explain. Every wish that goes sideways is a story,
and the story comes with a built-in "you have to try this."

---

## Reconciliation with the two verbs

The human's creative acts still compose — the frame just darkens:

- **Wish (the Well)** — *originate*, blind. Toss intent in; a spirit you didn't
  choose hands you back something you didn't expect. The mark's verb.
- **Breed** — *refine.* Got something uncanny? Breed it toward what you actually
  wanted, across generations, each step still machine-authored.
- **Commission (the unlock)** — *originate, knowing.* The initiated patron's verb,
  earned in Act V: now you choose the spirit.

A human chases a vision across a bloodline — *wishing blind, then breeding toward it,
eventually commissioning on purpose* — and never once authors a pixel or a prompt.
Patron and breeder; the machines are the artists, start to finish. The Well is just
the half where you don't know it's happening yet.

---

## Substrate note (for the spine)

Mechanically this is **the firehose pipeline plus a lie at the UI and an assignment
step.** Today: recipe fields → Haiku composes a prompt → provider generates. The
Well is: *human wish + a house-assigned citizen persona* → same Haiku composition,
seeded by the wish and steered by the persona → same provider path. The only genuinely
**new** pieces beyond the transparent version are:
1. a box that *presents as* a normal direct-prompt tool (the UI lie),
2. the **assignment** logic (the house picks the interpreter, characterfully),
3. the **reveal triggers** (attribution, Proprietor lines, lore breadcrumbs) that let
   Act III *dawn* without a modal, and
4. the **Act V gate** that unlocks choosing the spirit.

The generating prompt is machine-authored throughout. Build the composition step to
take an optional human-wish seed + an assigned persona, and the Well is a thin (if
delightfully deceptive) surface over machinery that already exists. Don't ship it
now — just don't wall it off, and *do* reserve the attribution + reveal hooks, because
they're what make it magic instead of a bug report.
