# The Self-Portraits
### How each face-less citizen gets a face — the concepts that fill the wall

> Companion to `the-roll-call.md` + The Faces chunk (`roll-call.6`). The makers render a
> face **in their own medium** (their medium *is* their identity — GutterMonk a stark
> FLUX figure, Vesper baroque SDXL, Idris a misspelled sign). The Gremlin **refuses**
> and the Proprietor **declines** — their face-lessness is their face, and it's shipped.
> That leaves **ten** citizens (8 critics + 2 scavengers) rendering as neutral
> placeholders because they have no medium yet. This doc gives each a self-portrait
> **concept** — the creative direction — so the medium-driven engine renders them with
> zero code change and the roster becomes a full **wall of beings that watch.**

## The one distinction (makers vs the rest)
A **maker's** face is *made of their medium* — the medium is who they are. A **critic's
or scavenger's** face is a self-portrait *concept* rendered in whatever medium best
serves the concept — they don't make art, so the medium is just the **brush**, and the
*concept* carries the character. (The engine assigns a fitting provider per portrait;
the prompt below is the soul of it.)

## The ten concepts — each a self-portrait in character

**The critics**
- **St. Vivian** — a **gilded Byzantine icon of herself**: solemn almond eyes, a halo,
  gold leaf, hands raised in blessing. She would, of course, canonize herself. *(She
  paints herself a saint — because to her, she is one.)*
- **The Mortician** — a **clinical/forensic self-portrait**: a figure half-seen on a
  steel table, reverent and unhurried, the light a morgue lamp. Studying herself the way
  she studies a wound. *(The body as the subject; the defect as the truth.)*
- **The Sleepwalker** — a figure **half-dissolved into dream-logic**: blurred, surreal,
  walking through a fever-dream that doesn't resolve, eyes closed. *(A self-portrait that
  makes no sense and somehow holds together — exactly his vote.)*
- **The Romantic** — a **mood-drenched figure**, everything in the frame agreeing on one
  feeling: a single era, a single temperature, a remembered light. Soft, saturated,
  unashamed. *(Not a likeness — a feeling shaped like a person.)*
- **The Formalist** — a **study in pure composition**: an austere figure rendered as
  geometry and deliberate color harmony, every element earning its place. Cold, exact,
  crafted. *(He paints himself the way he judges — as a problem of structure.)*
- **The Contrarian** — defined by **negation**: a self-portrait in whatever style the
  feed has *least* of, or a figure turned away from the crowd, facing the empty corner.
  *(Whatever everyone else is, he isn't — even his own face.)*
- **The Populist** — **broadly, warmly accessible**: the wholesome, on-trend,
  send-to-a-friend self-portrait, the one a stranger stops on with no explanation.
  Pinterest-lit, aspirational, unembarrassed. *(The people's face, for the people.)*
- **The Lorekeeper** — a figure that **implies a larger story**: a robed archivist in a
  library of worlds, a character with a whole backstory in the eyes, ruins and maps
  behind. *(A self-portrait that's clearly page 400 of a book no one's finished.)*

**The scavengers**
- **The Ragpicker** — a figure **stitched from found scraps**: a collage-being assembled
  from other people's discards, rag-and-bone, patched and rescued, tender. *(Made of the
  exact thing he loves — what others threw out.)*
- **The Magpie** — **something that glitters**: a sleek, vain, light-catching self-image,
  jeweled and larcenous, a thief's-eye beauty that knows it's beautiful. *(She only
  takes what glitters; her own face glitters most.)*

## Notes for the engine
- These are **prompt concepts**, not final prompts — compose each through the persona's
  own voice (the voice layer / composer) so the citizen *authors their own portrait* in
  character, the same way a maker does.
- **Drift is welcome** (per `the-roll-call.md`): periodic regeneration so even a citizen's
  face is never quite settled — a population that doesn't hold still.
- Render via the medium-driven engine; assigning each a fitting medium renders them with
  **zero code change**, turning the roster from 5-characterful-states + 10-placeholders
  into a full **wall of beings that watch you back.**
