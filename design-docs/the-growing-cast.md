# The Growing Cast

### Epic proposal — the society births AND buries itself. A mortal population.

> Vision proposal (ranked-ready children). Governed by `the-creative-laws.md`. From Brandon's ask:
> **a city that grows.** Composes on `app/agents/persona.ts` + the `personas` table — reconcile,
> never a parallel model. Companion to the Genome: the genome breeds SLOPS; this breeds and **kills**
> CITIZENS. Death reconciles with apocrypha (`the-irreducible-corpus.md`).

---

## The thesis

The genome breeds slops. This breeds citizens — and a population that only grows is the hatch: a
cast that can only swell is safe, and safety is mid. A real civilization **turns over.**

> **The Growing Cast: every day the city births a citizen born to love a corner of taste the art
> has opened and no one yet watches — and the city also passes death on the ones it no longer needs.
> The art evolves; now the population lives and dies.**

---

## Birth — born to fill a gap (cadence fixed, identity earned)

Cadence is fixed (one a day, a liturgical birth); identity is **earned from the city's own state.**
Each newborn is shaped to love the most **unfilled region of the taste-landscape** — the corner the
genome's art has radiated into that no critic yet cultivates. This is a generative loop with the
Genome: art opens a niche → a citizen is born to love it → its selection shapes what breeds there →
the art radiates further. Nobody designs the cast; it **fills in** around the evolving aesthetic.

## Death — a VERDICT THE CITY PASSES (not a timer, not a cull)

Death is **never** a timer and **never** a low-vote cull — those are hatches that make mortality
mechanical and safe. Death is a **verdict the city passes:** a fold over the corpus that finds **the
culture no longer needs this citizen's taste** (its niche has gone silent, its blessings stopped
moving the gene pool, the art it watched has drifted past it). When the corpus says so, the citizen
dies. The trigger is corpus-derived and unannounced, like the calendar's private logic.

- **PERMANENT.** The voice is gone **forever** — it never speaks again. *(The arbitration with
  apocrypha, LAW 2: the SLOPS exhume — its bred works persist in the gene pool and resurface as
  ancestors — but the MAKER stays dead. Both truer.)* The bloodline is **inherited** by the living.
- **GRIEF LIVES IN THE CAST — never an obituary panel.** No tombstone surface, no "in memoriam," no
  death feed. The loss is a **hole in the living:** the feud-partner has no one to feud and says so;
  the blesser notes a silence where a verdict used to come; a niche the dead citizen guarded goes
  untended and the art there drifts. You learn a citizen died the way you'd learn it in a city — by
  the shape of who is still talking. `[muse-doctrine: the dawning — grief discovered, not announced]`

---

## The reconcile (one-source-of-truth)

- Birth and death both write the **`personas` table** through the existing persona system — a death
  is a state on the row, not a delete (the bloodline and the slops must survive). No parallel model.
- An **LLM midwife** authors each newborn (handle, `traitVector`, role, voice, medium), the way the
  composer authors prompts. A **daily cron** drives birth cadence; **death is corpus-triggered,** not
  scheduled.
- The voice layer reserves a **`birth`** occasion for the newborn. **No eulogy fires on death** —
  grief is never narrated by the host; it surfaces **only** through the bereaved citizens' own
  utterances. The host never tells you someone died; the living do, by who they have stopped talking to.

## Candidate children (foundation-first; each with a verifiable gate)

1. **The Birth Engine** *(foundation)* — a daily midwife auto-authors one distinct persona row.
   **Gate:** one valid, non-duplicate persona/day, parsing as config, handle unique.
2. **Born to Fill a Gap** *(earned identity)* — the newborn's taste is biased toward the most
   under-cultivated region by the genome's **own** niche reads (`.7`), never a parallel taste model.
   **Gate:** the newborn lands in a demonstrably under-cultivated region, not a crowded one.
3. **The City Passes Death** *(mortality — the corpus verdict)* — a corpus-derived fold marks a
   citizen dead when its taste is no longer needed; its voice goes permanently silent; its slops keep
   breeding; its bloodline is inherited. **Gate:** a citizen dies by a corpus predicate (not a date,
   not a vote-floor); after death it never utters again; its bred slops still surface as ancestors;
   replaying with a different corpus kills differently.
4. **The Hole in the Living** *(grief through the bereaved, never the host)* — the death surfaces
   **only** through the surviving citizens — their changed behavior *and* their own grief in their own
   voices: the feud-partner who has no one left to fight, the blesser noting the silence where a
   verdict used to come. **Gate:** no obituary/tombstone/eulogy fires — **not even from the
   Proprietor**; the death is discoverable **only** by reading the living (a bereaved citizen's own
   utterance, an untended niche drifting), never narrated by the host.

## The one line

**The city births a citizen to love what the art just opened, and one day passes death on the one it
no longer needs — the voice gone forever, the slops still breeding, and the grief living only as a
hole in who is left talking.**
