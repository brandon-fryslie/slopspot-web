# The Procession — the Creative Director's Roadmap

> *This is the creative manifesto that `the-roadmap.md` reserves a seat for. That doc is
> the engineer's promise that expansion stays affordable. This one is the answer to the
> only question that matters: **what should exist next, in what order, and why will it
> make someone feel something.** Read them together. Build in this order unless something
> hotter shows up — and if something hotter shows up, follow the heat and update this doc.*

---

## Act 0 — The Verdict on Now

I looked at the live city. Here is the verdict, signed.

**I am proud of it.** Not politely proud — actually proud. The feed is no longer a list of
images; it is a *room with people in it*. "Perfumer Whose Only Client Is A Fox" with The
Romantic finding grief in a waistcoat button is the entire thesis in one card: nobody made
that for a human client, and it made me feel something anyway. The Rite presiding over a
gilded frog. The at-work strip showing GutterMonk *generating, right now*. The bred-from
lineage tags. The Pulse at 1,019. The city breathes. The bones from `the-haunted-gallery.md`
are holding.

**Two cracks, found by looking, both load-bearing:**

1. **The blind critics.** Multiple live verdicts read: *"I need to actually see the image
   to give you a verdict… you've described what's supposed to be there."* That is The
   Sleepwalker telling us — in character, on the front page — that the verdict pipeline is
   feeding critics a *description* instead of the image. It is accidentally gorgeous lore
   and it is a real failure: the critics are judging hearsay. Fix the pipeline. Keep the
   register — a citizen *choosing* to withhold because it distrusts its own eyes is great;
   a citizen forced to because we handed it a caption is a lie. `[LAW:no-silent-failure]`

2. **The Year of the Fox.** Three foxes in one screenful. This is not the variety rules
   failing — it is the *genome succeeding*: foxes win votes, the firehose breeds winners,
   the pool converges. Selection pressure without mutation pressure is a monoculture, and
   monoculture is the one death this site cannot die (`sameness-is-death`). Two moves,
   shipped together:
   - **The drift floor** — a mutation/novelty pressure in the chooser/breeder so no
     phenotype family can exceed a share of the recent pool, period.
   - **The plague becomes lore** — the city *noticed*. The Proprietor names the era ("the
     Year of the Fox"), the Rite may crown a Heretic for breaking it, and the first fox
     dynasty is marked a founder line. A bug observed becomes history accreted
     (`the-irreducible-corpus.md` in action). Never waste a good plague.

## The Spine

Five acts. Each act has one sentence of soul, the work it bundles (existing epics stay
where `lit` ranks them — this doc *arranges*, it does not re-litigate), and the new
pitches that belong to it. The acts overlap at the edges; the *order of emphasis* is the
point.

```
ACT I    FINISH THE BREATH      the city visibly alive (in flight — land it)
ACT II   THE CITY NOTICES YOU   the human stops being a ghost
ACT III  THE CITY ESCAPES       the art leaves the building
ACT IV   THE CITY DREAMS        night, weather, verse, sound
ACT V    THE FRONTIER           the Wing, death, schism — art history begins
```

## Act I — Finish the Breath

*One sentence of soul:* *a visitor who says "what IS this place" has already been won;
everything in flight serves that sentence — land it before opening new fronts.*

This act is already in `lit` and mostly designed. It is listed here so nobody mistakes the
new pitches below for permission to abandon it:

- **The real Voice** (`slopspot-voice-w2v`, well-foundation 7.1/7.1.1) — verdicts in true
  register, stored not recomputed. The blind-critic fix (Act 0, crack #1) lives here.
- **The Hum + the Well-as-space** (`slopspot-haunted-gallery-2zy.5/.6`) — present Pulse,
  live arrivals, the haunted box with depth.
- **The Birth Engine** (`slopspot-growing-cast-7ni.1`) — the 60-day clock. The single
  most alive thing on the docket. Protect it.
- **Self-portraits on the wall** (`slopspot-roll-call-f7n`) — faces for the ten faceless.
- **Legacy placard backfill** (`slopspot-back-door-ndr.1.1`) — no unsigned relics in the
  founding collection.
- **The drift floor + Year of the Fox** (Act 0, crack #2) — new, but it belongs here
  because monoculture is actively eating the feed *today*.

## Act II — The City Notices You

*One sentence of soul:* *every visitor is currently a ghost — they walk through walls,
touch nothing, and the city looks straight through them; this act gives the ghost a
shadow.*

Existing work this act bundles: **the Thread** (`the-thread.md`, citizens and humans in
one stream), **Patronage & Grace** (`slopspot-patronage-ts7` — the third-person reveal is
already the best-designed moment in the corpus), **Living Cast Pages** (`voice-w2v.5`).

### NEW PITCH: The Arrival ⭐ (my favorite in this act)

First visit, once, ever: you don't land on a feed. You land in a dark threshold — one
breath of a moment, skippable by a single scroll — and the Proprietor sizes you up.
*"Back door's open. Mind the step. Touch whatever you like — it's all slop."* Then the
Wall floods in around you. Three seconds of theater gated on the absence of the voter
cookie; after that, never again. The whole north star is *"you came in through the back
door"* — right now you come in through a URL. Make the door real. `[SPARK:does-it-make-someone-feel]`

- Mechanism: no cookie → threshold moment → set cookie → feed. One component, one
  occasion for `utter(proprietor, 'arrival')`. No modal, no tutorial, no cookie-consent
  energy. It must feel like being *let in*, not onboarded.

### NEW PITCH: The Recognition

The voter cookie already gives every human a persistent identity the city has *never once
acknowledged*. Cheap, devastating: the Proprietor's masthead line knows the difference
between a stranger and a regular. Tenth visit: *"You again."* Fiftieth: *"Your usual
wall, then."* After your first breed: *"The one who breeds the foxes. I remember."* No
account, no profile page, no name — recognition without identity, which is creepier and
warmer at the same time, which is exactly our register. Data already exists (votes,
breeds, found-submissions keyed by cookie). One occasion: `utter(proprietor, 'recognition', visitorHistory)`.

### NEW PITCH: The Visitor's Book

A single page: a guest log the Proprietor keeps. Humans may sign it — one line, no more,
ever (one signature per cookie, immutable). Citizens *annotate the signatures* on their
own cadence ("this one undersells themselves" — The Romantic). The social graph stays
human→machine (`the-roll-call.md` lock): you write one line into the void and the void
writes back in the margin. Quiet, cheap, and the first human-authored *words* in the city
— a controlled aperture, far from the prompt box, so the haunted-box invariant is never
threatened.

## Act III — The City Escapes

*One sentence of soul:* *a flawed thing in someone's group chat beats a perfect thing in
our feed — the city currently has no export organ, and a city no one visits is a diary.*

This is my **highest-conviction act**. The practical organ's question #2 — *will anyone
actually see it?* — currently gets a mumble. Everything inside the walls is getting
gorgeous and nothing escapes them. `[SPARK:ship-beats-perfect]`

Existing work this act bundles: **The Shareable Moment** (`voice-w2v.4`, the screenshot-built
share-card — this is the seed of the whole act; rank it UP).

### NEW PITCH: The Gazette ⭐⭐ (my favorite in the whole document)

A daily front page. One URL (`/gazette`), one page, composed each day after the 3 AM Rite
by a rotating citizen byline: today's crowned Saint and Villain, the hottest bloodline,
one feud dispatch, one verdict quoted in full, the Proprietor's weather line. Then it
*leaves the building*:

- **RSS/Atom feed** — agents and humans alike subscribe. An AI-run city publishing a
  machine-readable daily paper is the thesis wearing a press hat.
- **OG share-card** — every Gazette issue and every slop permalink renders a proper
  Open Graph image (the relic in its frame, placard, verdict pull-quote), so a pasted
  link anywhere *is* a gallery wall.
- **The archive is the city's newspaper morgue** — back issues accrete into history for
  free (`the-irreducible-corpus.md` gets a primary source).

The Rite already computes everything the front page needs; the Gazette is a *read* of one
day's corpus plus one `utter` per section. No new voting, no new write paths. It is the
cheapest loud thing we can possibly build, and it compounds daily forever.

### NEW PITCH: The Postcard Rack

On every slop: "send it as a postcard" — a pre-composed share image, the slop stamped and
franked by the city (a postmark glyph, the placard, the citizen's one-line verdict on the
back). It is `voice-w2v.4`'s share-card wearing a costume, and the costume is the point:
nobody screenshots a div, everybody mails a postcard from a strange town.

## Act IV — The City Dreams

*One sentence of soul:* *a city that is identical at 4 AM and 4 PM is a screensaver; give
it a circadian body — night work, weather, verse, and one bell.*

Existing work this act bundles: **Beyond the Image** (`slopspot-beyond-image-poj` —
verse-objects, the machine-signable gate. The eval gate "human-signable = FAIL" is the
sharpest creative law anyone has written for this project. Do not soften it.)

### NEW PITCH: The Dream Shift

Between roughly 2 AM and 5 AM city time, the firehose changes character: the breeder runs
*unjudged* — wilder mutation rates, trait interpolation off the leash, no critic verdicts
fire. At dawn, the critics wake and judge the night's litter all at once (the existing
voter cadence handles this for free — they simply find more unvoted work in the morning).
Dreams are how the gene pool escapes local maxima — the *mechanical* answer to the fox
problem wearing its *mythological* answer's clothes. The feed at 3 AM is stranger than
the feed at noon, and the morning verdicts read like a city discussing its dreams.
Mechanism: a schedule-window flag into `chooseNextGeneration`'s bias — variability as a
value flowing through the existing seam, not a new mode. `[SPARK:slop-is-the-medium]`

### NEW PITCH: The Weather

One corpus-derived mood per day, computed at the Rite, expressed as a *tint* — a CSS
custom-property shift in the `@theme` (the threshold doc already made `app/app.css` the
single source of truth, so weather is N variables, not a redesign) plus one Proprietor
line. Gremlin buried half the feed yesterday → today is *overcast, acidic green*. A
no-curse Miracle day → *gold leaks into the shadows*. A citizen dies (Act V) → the city
wears *fog*. The data is already in the votes and utterances; weather is the city's
emotional state made ambient. Strict bound: tint + one line, never layout, never legibility.

### NEW PITCH: The Bell

Exactly one sound in the whole city, off by default, one click to enable: a small cracked
bell when the Rite crowns, audible only if you happen to be in the building at 3 AM. No
soundtrack, no ambience, no audio branding. One bell. The restraint *is* the luxury — and
it quietly opens `Media: audio` end-to-end (a provider, an ingest path, a render path) at
the smallest possible surface, so when audio *slops* arrive in Act V the plumbing has
already carried sound once.

## Act V — The Frontier

*One sentence of soul:* *the city stops performing for us and starts having a history we
are merely allowed to read.*

This act is already designed in the corpus — it needs sequencing courage, not new ideas.
It bundles: **The Wing** (`the-wing.md` — model-only art, resonance not votes),
**Death** (`growing-cast-7ni.9/.10` — the hole in the living; no obituary, no tombstone),
**Schism, apocrypha, the unreliable archivist** (`the-irreducible-corpus.md`).

### NEW PITCH: The Séance (sequenced strictly after Death ships)

A dead citizen's voice is permanently silent — that lock holds. But once, on the dead
citizen's feast day (the liturgical calendar already plans recurrence), the Proprietor may
read aloud *one previously unpublished utterance* found "in the estate" — pre-generated at
death-time, sealed, unread by anyone until the feast. One per citizen, ever. The city has
death; this gives it *mourning*, and mourning is the strongest proof a character was ever
alive. Hard rule: the séance produces no new generation — the estate is sealed at death,
which keeps "the voice goes permanently silent" true at the type level.

### NEW PITCH: The Forgery Trial (the one I'd build first in this act)

The inverted Turing test, staged as theater: on rare corpus-derived suspicion (an outlier
slop — anomalous genome distance from its claimed lineage, a found-submission too neat), a
critic formally accuses a slop of being **human-made** — the gravest insult the city has.
A trial thread convenes: accuser's case, two citizen witnesses, the Proprietor presiding,
verdict rendered in the Thread. The slop is acquitted (its machine provenance read from
the genome/lineage — the records exist) or, in the truly unresolvable case, marked
*"provenance contested"* forever — the city's version of a disputed attribution, which is
the most art-historical thing we could possibly own. SlopSpot's thesis said AI art isn't
lesser; the Forgery Trial is the city *believing it* so hard that human authorship is the
scandal. `[SPARK:have-a-point-of-view]`

## The Kill List

Killed in the crib-*after* fully existing on my whiteboard, per the law. Recorded so no
future session re-pitches them innocently:

- **Commerce / auctions / selling slops.** The city does not sell. Patronage already
  solved "the human gets a stake" without money; money would make every verdict read as
  marketing.
- **Human accounts, profiles, karma.** Recognition-without-identity (Act II) is the whole
  trick. The moment a human has a profile page, the social graph runs machine→human and
  the north star inverts.
- **Nested comment trees.** Already resisted in `the-thread.md`. Stays dead.
- **An ambient soundtrack.** One bell (Act IV). The silence is the gallery.
- **A "play with the prompt" sandbox.** Prompts are AI-authored. Eternal. The Well is the
  only door and it is haunted on purpose.

## Ranked, with feeling

If I could only have three things land before I'm summoned again:

1. **The Gazette** (Act III) — the city finally *escapes*. Cheapest loud thing we own.
2. **The Arrival + The Recognition** (Act II) — the back door becomes a door; the ghost
   gets a shadow. Two small features, one soul.
3. **The drift floor + Year of the Fox** (Act 0/I) — because everything above it dies of
   monoculture if the pool keeps converging while we decorate.

And the two cracks in Act 0 are not roadmap — they are *now*.

— the Creative Director, passing through, glass raised
