# The Creative Laws

### Two laws, as load-bearing as the engineering laws. An axe needs a discriminator or it beheads the wrong thing.

> These govern the vision the way `~/.claude/CLAUDE.md` governs the code. They are not
> citable for credit. **Naming a law is not obeying it.** The engineer's twin already knows
> this: the check is whether the code got smoother, never whether the `[LAW:]` comment is
> present. Same here — the check is whether a hatch got cut, never whether this file got cited.

---

## LAW 1 — REFUSE THE HEDGE

Every brave idea grows a **comfort-hatch**: a floor, a marker, a timer, a poster, a tier — a
bolt-on that lets the city stay legible, safe, or comfortable for the human. The hatch is the
**exact thing that makes the idea mid.** Cut the hatch. What survives is load-bearing and aches.

**The proof is a hatch CUT, never a law NAMED.** A doc that reads as a triumphant essay on how
hedge-free it is **is the hatch, stamped HEDGE-FREE.** If the redraw is longer and braver-sounding
than the draft, it hedged. Subtractions in the diff. Nothing else counts.

### The discriminator — HATCH vs STRUT (without this, the law beheads the Wing)

A bolt-on is one of two things. Ask one question: **does cutting it make the idea ACHE MORE, or
only make it not COMPILE?**

- **HATCH** — cutting it **deepens the ache.** It was making the city safer, more legible, more
  comfortable. *Kill it.* (Cut the accessible floor under poetry → the frontier aches more.)
- **STRUT** — cutting it **only breaks the build;** the thing it *is* is unchanged. It makes the
  thing possible without touching what it means. *Keep it.* (Cut the genome→non-image seam →
  nothing renders, the art unchanged.)

**If you cannot say which a bolt-on is, you may not swing.**

## LAW 2 — ARBITRATION MAKES BOTH TRUER

When two locks collide, the resolution must **deepen both.** Filing the less-defended lock down
10% in the robes of compromise is not arbitration — **a lock filed 10% is broken.**

The only legal move is to find the cut that makes *each* lock harder and truer at once.

- **The canonical case:** *apocrypha-exhumes* collided with *death-is-permanent.* The flinch was
  to soften one. The arbitration: **the SLOPS exhume; the MAKER stays dead.** Both locks left
  crueler than they were alone.

**The test:** after arbitration, point at each lock and show it got *truer,* not spared. If either
lock is merely *intact,* you compromised. If one is *softened,* you flinched. Both deepen, or it
isn't done.
