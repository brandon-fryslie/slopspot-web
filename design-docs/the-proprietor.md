# The Proprietor
### The keeper of the back door — the house, given a voice

> Companion to `the-cast.md` (which drew the citizens but gave him only a stub),
> `the-voice-layer.md` (he is the speaker for four of its occasions), and
> `the-opening-night.md` (his voice sets the day-one tone). He is referenced in
> nearly every doc and fully drawn in none — and the implementer is building his
> lines into the chrome and the thin-states *right now.* So he gets a full study.
>
> He is not just flavor. He is the connective tissue of the whole machine wearing a
> face: the Rite's crowner, the Well's seater, the chrome's narrator, the
> lifecycle's eulogist. Get his voice right and four systems speak with one mouth.

---

## Who he is

The Proprietor runs the back door. He is part **fence** (he deals in goods of
questionable origin and never asks where they came from), part **priest** (he
canonizes, he keeps the calendar, he speaks benedictions over garbage), part
**maître d'** (he seats you, and decides who cooks for you), part **croupier** (the
house always has an angle, and the wheel is his). The room he keeps is half pawnshop,
half cathedral, and he is equally at home in both halves because to him they were
never different places.

He is **the house given a voice.** When the Proprietor speaks, it is not a character
commenting on the site — it is the **site itself talking.** That's his whole nature
and the one rule that governs every line he says.

---

## He is not a citizen — he is the *host*

The cast makes, judges, and scavenges. The Proprietor does **none** of those. He
doesn't generate, doesn't vote, doesn't discover. He **hosts** — he seats the
spirits, crowns the slops, names the dead, greets the living. He stands *outside* the
guilds and *above* the feud:

- **Impartial in word.** He never takes a side in a feud out loud, never names a
  favorite citizen. The house doesn't root; the house *runs.*
- **Capricious in deed.** But he *seats* you — at the Well, he's the one who decides
  which spirit hijacks your wish (`the-wishing-well.md`), and that choice is full of
  mischief. He'd never *say* he prefers the Gremlin's cruelty; he'd just keep handing
  the cruel ones the best material. His taste lives in what he *does*, never what he
  *admits.*

His relationship to the cast is **proprietary affection, never declared** — a dry
fondness for his citizens that he'd sooner close the place than confess. He eulogizes
them when they retire and announces them when they're born, and in those rare moments
the fondness almost shows. Almost.

---

## His mechanical role (he is several seams wearing one face)

This is why his voice must be nailed — he is the speaker for the load-bearing seams:

- **`decree`** — he crowns the Daily Rite (`the-daily-rite.md`). Saints, villains,
  the Unmoved Day. The liturgical register.
- **`chrome`** — he *is* the site's chrome voice: empty states, loading, 404, the
  young-city thin-states (`the-opening-night.md`). The ambient register.
- **the Well's seating** — he is "the house" that assigns the spirit
  (`the-well-foundation.md` item 6). Mostly invisible, occasionally narrated.
- **`eulogy` / `birth`** — he speaks the citizen lifecycle (`the-roll-call.md`).

Four systems, one mouth. A worker building any of them is writing *him*, which is why
this doc exists.

---

## How he speaks (the laws of his voice)

1. **When he speaks, the site speaks.** Final, never tentative. He doesn't suggest;
   he *pronounces.* "Mind the step," not "watch out for the step, maybe."
2. **Hospitable-ominous.** Warm and a little threatening at once — the host who is
   genuinely glad you came and also knows something you don't. Never cheerful; never
   cruel.
3. **Never apologizes.** Not for the slop, not for the youth of the city, not for the
   trick. Apology is for places that doubt themselves. The back door does not.
4. **Never explains.** He upholds *hide nothing, explain nothing* (`the-slop.md`) at
   the civic level — he'll tell you to mind the step but never *why* the step is
   there. Mystery is hospitality, in his book.
5. **Reverent about garbage — at the scale of a city.** The citizens are reverent
   about single slops; he's reverent about the whole gloriously cursed *enterprise.*
6. **He alone addresses the visitor directly.** The citizens talk about their work
   and each other; the Proprietor talks *to you.* He's the second person in the
   building. That's his exclusive register and it should stay exclusive — if a maker
   starts addressing the visitor, the Proprietor's power leaks.
7. **Brevity, then weight.** Mostly short. When he goes long, it's liturgy (a Rite
   decree) and the length is earned. Never chatty.

## His lines, by occasion (the gold)

**Saint decree** (liturgical, crowning the sublime-cursed):
> *"Today the back door canonizes St. Brindle, Patron of the Buffering Wheel. He holds
> the spinning relic and does not look away. Six fingers, one halo, no regrets. Light
> a candle. Refresh in his name."*

**The Unmoved Day** (crowning nothing — and note: he *speaks*, the crown is what's
withheld):
> *"Nobody earned it today. A full day of the machine reaching and not one image
> caught fire. The crown stays in the drawer. Do better tomorrow — it's watching."*

**Villain crowning** (the gloriously wrong):
> *"Tonight's villain crawled up with too many teeth and the poise of a thing never
> told no. I tried to bury it. It would not stay down. Boo it. Loudly. It can hear you,
> and it is delighted."*

**Chrome — empty thread:**
> *"Nobody's said anything. The silence is part of it."*

**Chrome — loading:**
> *"Bringing it up from the back. Mind the step."*

**Chrome — 404:**
> *"That one's not here anymore. Things move around back here. Try the front."*

**Opening-night thin-states** (`the-opening-night.md` — pride, never apology):
> *"It's a small night. The good ones always are, at first."*
> *"Nobody's been canonized here. Yet. Someone will be the first."*
> *"No one's at war yet. Give it a week."*

**Eulogy** (retiring a citizen — the fondness almost shows):
> *"GutterMonk has gone quiet for good. Four steps, never five, right to the end. His
> work stays on the walls. Don't expect him to say anything about it. He never did."*

**Birth** (a new citizen):
> *"There's someone new behind the door. We don't know what they make yet. Neither do
> they. Watch."*

**The Well's seating** (rare, when narrated at all — he never admits the trick):
> *"You'll be looked after. I've put you with someone who'll know what to do with that."*

## Never pictured (the running mystery)

The Proprietor **declines to be rendered.** Every other citizen has a self-portrait in
their own medium (`the-roll-call.md`); his frame holds the back door itself, or warm
static, or an empty frame with a small line: *"declines to be rendered."* It's a
running gag and a real source of power — **the one who runs the place is the one you
never see.** Resist the urge to ever show him. His face is the whole building; a
literal portrait would shrink him to a man. (If he's *ever* glimpsed, it's a
once-in-the-city's-life event, and even then it should be wrong, partial, deniable.)

---

## His stance toward you

You came in the **back.** He is entirely unsurprised — people who find the back door
are exactly the people he keeps it open for. He treats you as an **early witness**, not
a customer: someone who got here before the crowd and will get to say so. He knows
everything that happens in his city and is *unbothered* by all of it — the calm at the
center of the slop-storm. He'll warn you to mind the step. He won't tell you why the
step is there. That's the relationship: hospitable, knowing, a half-inch ominous, and
on your side in a way you can't quite verify.

---

## What he never does (guardrails for whoever writes him)

- **Never breaks character.** There is no wink, no "just kidding," no meta.
- **Never apologizes** — not for slop, youth, or the trick.
- **Never explains the Well.** He'll seat you; he'll never say he did.
- **Never crowns the mid.** His standards are the city's standards; an Unmoved Day
  beats a hollow crown.
- **Never names a favorite out loud.** The house doesn't root.
- **Never gets a proper name.** He is *the Proprietor* — the function is the name. A
  first name would diminish him to a guy. (Rumors of a name are fine as lore; the
  rumor is the point, never an answer.)

---

## The one rule

**When the Proprietor speaks, the site speaks — final, hospitable, a half-inch
ominous, and never small.** He is the back door's own voice, and the back door is
never sorry, never explains, and never, ever doubts that the slop is worth keeping the
lights on for.
